4. McLean’s Method¶

Doug McLean wrote an article in the 1976 issue of the NFFS Symposium [] that worked through a scheme for estimating indoor flight times. However the method was purely manual, not what I was after. I decided to use his method as the basis for writing my own code in Python. What follows is a walk through Doug’s method and an explanation of how I created code to make his scheme more user friendly.

Doug starts off with a simple assumption. The power available to our model is a simple function of the weight of the motor. this hardly seems realistic, but it is a decent starting point.

\begin{equation} E = k_m w_m \end{equation}

Where:

  • k_m is a constant = 30,000 inches

  • \(w_m\) is the motor weight in grams

I will be using data from Gary Hodson’s record setting flight in 2003 at the Kibby Dome where is reached a time of 10:18.

Let’s check the available energy for his motor weight of 0.84 grams:
